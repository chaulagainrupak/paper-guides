## What Is KUCAT?

Almost every program at KU (with one exception, see below) requires you to sit the **Kathmandu University Common Admission Test (KUCAT)**, delivered as a **Computer-Based Test (CBT)**. You'll take it in one of three subject groups, **PCM**, **PCB**, or **PMCs**, depending on the program you're applying to.

## Eligibility Criteria

You need to have completed (or be finishing) 10+2 or equivalent, with at least **2.0 GPA or 50% aggregate marks**. The exact requirement depends on which board you're coming from:

**+2 / NEB students:** Minimum GPA of 2.0 or 50% aggregate marks or equivalent.

**A-Level students:** Minimum of 3 A-Level subjects and 1 AS-Level subject, in a PCB, PCM, or PMCs combination. A-Levels gives out a Percentage Uniform Mark (PUM) in your statement of results. You need an aggregate of 50%, meaning the average of your 3 subjects (excluding the English general paper) needs to come out to 50% or higher. If your statement doesn't show a PUM, you need at least a Grade D across PCB/PCM/PMCs instead, since a Grade D is roughly guaranteed to put you above that 50% mark anyway.

**IB Diploma students:** Minimum of 6 subjects in a PCB, PCM, or PMCs combination, with a final grade of at least 3 in each course individually.

**CTEVT Diploma holders:** Eligible for related disciplines, subject to equivalent percentage criteria.

**Foreign boards:** You'll need to present an equivalence certificate from KU at the time of admission.

One important note if you're from a Biology background: **if you want to sit the PCM group, you need to have taken and passed Additional Mathematics.** This applies across the board, regardless of which board you studied under.

Candidates still waiting on their Grade 12 results can apply for **provisional admission**, as long as they meet the other requirements.

## Programs and Test Groups

This is the part most students get confused about. Not every program needs the same test group. Here's the actual breakdown.

### PCM Only

**School of Engineering:**

- BE in Electrical and Electronics Engineering (Communications)
- BE in Electrical and Electronics Engineering (Power & Control)
- BE in Mechanical Engineering
- BE in Civil Engineering
- BE in Chemical Engineering
- BE in Mining Engineering
- BE in Environmental Engineering

These require PCM in **both** Grade XI and XII (or equivalent).

### PCM or PMCs

**School of Engineering:**

- BE in Computer Engineering
- BE in Geomatics Engineering\*
- Bachelor of Architecture\*\*
- Bachelor of Information Technology\*\*\*
- BTech in Artificial Intelligence

**School of Science:**

- BSc in Computer Science
- BSc in Computational Mathematics
- BSc in Applied Physics
- Bachelor of Data Science

### PCM or PCB

**School of Science:**

- BTech in Biotechnology
- BSc in Environmental Science
- BSc in Bioinformatics
- BSc in Agriculture

### No KUCAT Required

**Bachelor in Health Informatics** is the one exception. Instead of sitting the CBT, you'll need to consult directly with the [Department of Health Informatics](https://dohi.ku.edu.np) for their admission procedure.

- Geomatics Engineering applicants who want to be considered for scholarships need to fill a separate scholarship form after the CBT rank-based list is published.
- Architecture applicants must also pass an aptitude test, conducted after the CBT rank-based list is published.
- BIT is only offered through KU-affiliated colleges, not the main campus.

## CBT Centers

You can sit the exam at any of these centers, pick whichever is most convenient:

1. Dhulikhel
2. Lalitpur
3. Biratnagar
4. Rupandehi (Manigram)
5. Pokhara

Keep in mind you **can't change your center after the application deadline**, so choose carefully when you apply.

## Application Fee

The processing fee is **NRs. 2,000**, and it's **non-refundable and non-transferable**. Payment is accepted **only via eSewa (ePay)**, no other payment methods are listed.

## FAQ

**Do I need to pick PCM or PCB before I apply?**
Yes, your test group depends on the program you're applying to. Check the breakdown above; some programs (like BSc Computer Science) accept either PCM or PMCs, while others (like Biotechnology) accept PCM or PCB.

**I'm from the Biology stream, can I still sit PCM?**
Yes, but only if you've taken and passed Additional Mathematics. Without it, you won't be eligible for the PCM group.

**My A-Level results don't show a PUM, am I still eligible?**
Yes. If your statement of results doesn't include a Percentage Uniform Mark, KU falls back to a grade requirement instead: you need at least a Grade D across your PCB/PCM/PMCs subjects.

**What if my Grade 12 results aren't out yet?**
You can still apply for provisional admission, as long as you've already passed Grade 11 (or equivalent) and meet the other eligibility criteria.

**Can I switch my CBT center after applying?**
No. Center changes aren't allowed once the application deadline has passed, so pick the center you're sure about.

**Does Bachelor in Health Informatics need the CBT too?**
No, it's the one program that skips KUCAT entirely. You'll need to contact the Department of Health Informatics directly for their process.

**How much is the application fee, and how do I pay it?**
NRs. 2,000, payable only through eSewa (ePay). It's non-refundable.
